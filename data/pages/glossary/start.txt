====== Glossary ======

Below are the most commonly used terms within miraX and related workflows.

----

===== Dashboard =====
A visual interface used to display maps, charts, indicators, and analytical results.

<WRAP tip>
Dashboards help users monitor data and interact with visual analytics in real time.
</WRAP>

----

===== STD (Single Time Series Dashboard) =====
A dashboard focused on displaying a single time series dataset.

Examples:
  * Water level at one station
  * Rainfall at one sensor
  * Groundwater evolution over time

<WRAP info>
STD dashboards are useful for detailed analysis of one monitoring location.
</WRAP>

----

===== MTD (Multiple Time Series Dashboard) =====
A dashboard that displays and compares multiple time series simultaneously.

Examples:
  * Comparing rainfall stations
  * River discharge at different locations
  * Multi-sensor monitoring

<WRAP info>
MTD dashboards are commonly used for regional analysis and comparison.
</WRAP>

----

===== Network =====
A data source or collection of connected monitoring stations providing time series data.

Examples:
  * Meteorological stations
  * Groundwater sensors
  * River gauges

----

===== WCS (Web Coverage Service) =====
A geospatial web service standard used to provide raster or gridded spatial data.

Typical uses:
  * Elevation models
  * Satellite imagery
  * Raster-based simulations

<WRAP important>
WCS services provide downloadable spatial coverage data rather than only map visualization.
</WRAP>

----

===== Subsidence =====
The gradual sinking or lowering of the ground surface, commonly caused by peat oxidation, groundwater extraction, or soil compaction.

In miraX, subsidence analysis is used to:
  * Simulate peatland degradation
  * Estimate surface lowering
  * Evaluate CO₂ emission reduction scenarios

<WRAP alert>
Peatland subsidence is often accelerated when groundwater levels decrease.
</WRAP>
